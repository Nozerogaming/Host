Under the hood

Why KVM instead of containers

Container-based "VPS" plans oversell a shared kernel — one busy neighbor and your CPU wait time climbs. KVM gives every instance its own virtualized kernel and a hard resource ceiling, so what you provision is what you get, every hour of the month.

  • Dedicated vCPU cores, never oversold
  • Your own kernel — install any supported OS
  • NVMe-backed storage with predictable IOPS
  • Anti-DDoS filtering at the network edge
